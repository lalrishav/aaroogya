ABOUT FELLOWSHIP


The Aaroogya Research & Public Health Fellowship (ARPHF) Fellowship is helping to shape the next generation of leaders committed to positive change while also strengthening civil society in India. Apply now

The Aaroogya Research & Public Health Fellowship (ARPHF) for Service in India is an immersive, 7-month and 3-month volunteer service program matching young professionals with development sector....

The Aaroogya Research & Public Health Fellowship (ARPHF) for Service in India is an immersive, 7-month and 3-month volunteer service program matching young professionals with development sector. Fellows work on scalable and sustainable development projects in the fields of public health and research for preventive healthcare. Creating a global pool of future leaders and nurture these talented change makers in the space of social innovation and public health in India.

Through collaboration and capacity-building, Fellows, Vitrana Healthcare, Rotary India, Aaroogya, and civil society leaders form dynamic partnerships to capacity-build, exchange knowledge and skills, and share a mutual passion for advancing social and economic development. Fellows serve on an annual basis and semi – annual basis from January 1st to July 31st and January 1st to April 31st respectively with Vitrana Healthcare, Rotary India, and Aaroogya, across India.

COURSE DETAILS

Course Name : Grphic Design & Multimedia

The Fellowship creates a community of socially engaged, global citizens dedicated to positive change. Together they represent a collective force for sustaining a long-term agenda dedicated towards change in India, and beyond. The ARPHF Fellowship provides a renowned platform to develop the skills, resources, and network needed to become an inclusive and socially-minded change maker. India has always been a densely complex demography with diversity and inclusive heterogeneity. Healthcare has been a misnomer with absence of the concept of Preventive Healthcare in India. Public Health is the field of utmost importance to start with a healthy country dream. With the launch of Ayushman Bharat, in India, a new vigour infused in the space of Primarily making healthcare accessible and making early diagnosis of diseases. Combating healthcare epidemics by converging nutrition, sanitation, hygiene, general health and cancer screening in rural India is a comprehensive approach to being able to be a part of the big renaissance in making India Healthy. The Fellows will be the first batch of Public Health change makers and social innovative leaders in this space and represent the true revolution in Indian Healthcare space Globally.


INSTRUCTOR

Head Of The Department : Ara’af Imtiaz

Fellows will be trained on-ground and off-ground through skills-sharing, training, and capacity-building. Responsibilities include On-ground - research primary research and data analytics, marketing and Public health sessions. Offground - vary based on a Fellow’s skills, experience, and interests. The fellows will be experiencing training sessions by globally renowned mentors and training with skill development and capacity building organizations crating Impact globally.

Project requirement also include: program design and implementation, monitoring and evaluation, graphic design, videography, blog writing, data collection and visualization, developing digital content, event planning, qualitative and quantitative research, creative writing, curriculum design, fundraising, teaching, training teachers, community organizing, and marketing and communications.

Each Fellow is paired with a project supervisor and staff member for support throughout the project. During the selection process, we take note of the skill sets and career aspirations of potential Fellows to match them with set notion of prerequisites.
